# Daily Health Score — Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** May 28, 2026

**Developer:** Matthew Curtis Fieleke (“we,” “us,” or “the developer”)

**App:** Daily Health Score (native iOS application)

This policy describes how **Daily Health Score** handles information when you use the app. It is written for App Store users and privacy reviewers.

---

## Summary

- The app reads **sleep**, **dietary fiber**, and **exercise minutes** from **Apple Health** (with your permission).
- Scores and history are stored **on your device**.
- We do **not** require an account, and this version does **not** upload your health data to our servers.
- You can **export** or **clear** local data in **Settings**.

---

## Information the app accesses

### Apple Health (HealthKit)

With your explicit permission, the app reads:

| Health type | Purpose |
|-------------|---------|
| Sleep Analysis (asleep) | Calculate sleep hours and sleep score for each day |
| Dietary Fiber | Calculate fiber intake and fiber score for each day |
| Apple Exercise Time | Calculate exercise minutes and exercise score for each day |

The app uses HealthKit’s read-only access for these types. It does not write health data to Apple Health unless a future version clearly states otherwise in the app and this policy.

**You control access** through iOS: **Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → Daily Health Score**.

### Data stored on your device

The app stores locally, on your iPhone:

- Daily scores and metric values derived from Health data
- Your goal settings (sleep hours target, fiber target, etc.)
- Suggestions shown for each day
- Optional SMART goals and check-in state
- App preferences (e.g. rotation of encouragement/motivation text)

Local retention is limited to approximately **125 days** of daily records, after which older records may be removed by the app’s storage rules.

### Notifications (optional)

If you enable notifications for the app, iOS may deliver **local reminders** related to SMART goals you create. Notification permission is optional and controlled in iOS Settings.

---

## What we do not collect

In the current version of the app:

- We do **not** require you to create an account.
- We do **not** operate a cloud backend that receives your Health metrics.
- We do **not** sell your personal information.
- We do **not** use third-party advertising or analytics SDKs in the app for cross-app tracking (as of this policy date).

---

## How information is used

Information is used only to:

- Display your daily score and metric breakdown
- Show rolling 7 / 30 / 90-day summaries
- Suggest a primary focus and daily tips
- Support optional SMART goals and reminders
- Let you export or delete data you control in Settings

---

## Sharing of information

We do **not** share your Health data with third parties for marketing.

**Apple** provides HealthKit and processes data according to Apple’s policies when you use Apple Health on your device.

If you use **export** in Settings, you choose where exported files go (e.g. Files, email, AirDrop). That transfer is under your control.

---

## Data retention and deletion

- **On device:** Records are kept for about 125 days, then pruned according to app logic.
- **Delete:** Use in-app **Settings** to clear local app data.
- **Revoke Health access:** Use iOS Health privacy settings; the app will no longer read new Health data.

Uninstalling the app removes app-local data from your device subject to iOS behavior and backups.

---

## Children’s privacy

Daily Health Score is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided information through the app, contact us and we will help address it.

---

## Security

Health data is processed on your device. Protect your iPhone with a passcode, Face ID, or Touch ID. We cannot guarantee security of data if your device is compromised or if you share exported files.

---

## International users

The app is designed for use on your device. If you use the app outside your home country, Apple Health and local laws may still apply to how you manage health information on your device.
